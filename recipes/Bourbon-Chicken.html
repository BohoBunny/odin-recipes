Bourbon Chicken

Description

Bourbon Chicken is a staple in southern cuisine from Virginia to Louisiana its a family favorite that graces most tables. With sweet brown sugar, savory soy sauce, iconic bourbon his quick and easy dish is sure to have everyone wanting seconds without breaking the bank.

This bourbon Chicken uses real bourbon for taste only and chicken thighs to get a more tender meat. If you want a more spicy dish you can add old bay to sub for red pepper flakes and Sriracha sauce. While not required a Wok works best for this kind of dish. If you don't have a wok or dutch oven you can you a large frying pan instead.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • boneless Chicken thighs
  • 1/2 cup packed brown sugar
  • 3/8 cup bourbon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 2 cloves garlic minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon crushed red pepper flakes
  • 3 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 4 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  1. Cut the chicken into 1 inch pieces and in a large bowl toss it with 2 tablespoons of the cornstarch, salt, and pepper.
  2. Add 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil into an wok on high heat and let it heat to sizzle then add the first piece of chicken.
  3. Cook until brown rotating chicken every 3 minutes.
  4. Remove the chicken from the wok and add in minced garlic. Cook for 30 seconds then add bourbon, ground ginger, brown sugar, red pepper flakes, and soy sauce to Wok.
  5. Bring to a boil then add back in the chicken cook for 9-13 minutes then add remaining corn starch and stir.
  6. Reduce heat and cover, cook until sauce is thickened then its ready to serve.
